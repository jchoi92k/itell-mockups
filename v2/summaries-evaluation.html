What is a page summary?

At the end of certain pages, iTELL asks you to write a short summary of what you just read. This helps reinforce your understanding and gives you immediate feedback on whether you captured the key ideas.

Not every page requires a summary. Your textbook's page configuration determines which pages have summary activities.

How to write a summary

1

Read the entire page

Scroll through all the content. Blurred sections will unlock as you read.

2

Find the summary box

At the bottom of the page, you'll see a text area labeled "Write your summary here."

3

Write your summary

Summarize the main ideas of the page in your own words. Aim for 50–200 words.

4

Click Submit

Your summary will be scored automatically. You'll see feedback within a few seconds.

Summary requirements

A successful submission must:

  • Be between 50 and 200 words
  • Be written in English
  • Be on topic — related to the page content
  • Not be plagiarized — use your own words

How your summary is scored

Your summary is automatically evaluated on these attributes:

Content

Includes main points, supporting details, and overall organization.

Key Words

Uses important terms and phrases from the text appropriately.

If your summary scores well, you can move to the next section. If it scores low, you'll need to revise and resubmit.

Tip: If you're stuck after multiple attempts, iTELL AI will suggest specific passages to re-read to improve your understanding.

End-of-page activities in your textbook

Depending on which textbook you're using, you may have additional activities beyond summaries. Select your textbook using the dropdown above to see what applies to you.

Constructed Response Items (CRI)

In addition to summaries, some pages include Constructed Response Items — short-answer questions embedded within the text. These appear as yellow-highlighted question boxes as you read.

  • Type your answer in the provided text field
  • Click "Submit" to check your response
  • You'll receive immediate feedback from iTELL AI

Cloze Tests (Fill-in-the-blank)

Some pages use cloze tests instead of summaries as the end-of-page activity. In a cloze test, you fill in missing words from a passage.

  • Read the passage with blanks
  • Select or type the correct word for each blank
  • All blanks must be filled before you can submit
Whether a page uses a summary or cloze test is configured by your instructor. You can't switch between them.

End-of-Page Quizzes

In this textbook, some pages end with a multiple-choice quiz instead of (or in addition to) a summary. Quizzes test your recall of specific facts and concepts from the page.

  • Read all quiz questions carefully
  • Select your answer for each question
  • Click "Submit Quiz" to see your results
  • You may need to score above a threshold to proceed
This textbook does not use Constructed Response Items or Cloze tests. Your end-of-page activities are summaries and quizzes.
