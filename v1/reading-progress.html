Understanding the Page Layout

The textbook's contents are divided into Pages. Each page is part of a Module (chapter), and you navigate between them using the left-side navigation bar. Here's what you'll see on a typical page:

  1. Top navigation bar — Shows your current progress including the Page and Chunk headers.
  2. Left navigation bar — Links to different Chapters and Pages within the Module. Pages with checkmarks are completed; pages with locks require you to complete the preceding page first.
  3. Assignment link — Jumps to the bottom of the page where the summary-writing activity is located.
  4. Reset button — Resets the progress for the current page.
  5. Back to top link — Returns you to the top of the page.
  6. iTELL AI chat — Opens the AI assistant in the bottom right corner.
  7. Notes indicator — Shows a count of notes you've made on the current page.

Unlocking Content

When you first open a page, most of the content will be blurred. This is by design — iTELL reveals content progressively as you read.

1

Read the visible section

Start by reading the unblurred text at the top of the page.

2

Click "Continue Reading"

When you reach the end of the visible section, click the red Continue Reading button to unblur the next section.

3

Answer questions if prompted

Sometimes instead of a Continue Reading button, you'll see a Constructed Response Item. Answer the question to unlock the next section. See Questions & Cloze for details.

Tip: You cannot skip ahead. Content is unlocked sequentially to ensure you engage with each section before moving on.

Navigation Elements

The left sidebar shows your overall progress through the textbook. Each page is represented as a link with status indicators:

  • Checkmark — You've completed this page.
  • Lock icon — This page is locked. Complete the preceding page to unlock it.
  • No icon — This page is available and not yet completed.

Completing a Page

To complete a page and unlock the next one, you must:

  1. Read through all content sections (clicking Continue Reading or answering questions to unblur each one).
  2. Answer all Constructed Response Items (short answer questions).
  3. Complete the end-of-page activity — either a Summary or a Cloze Test depending on the page.
You will not be able to proceed to the next page without successfully completing all tasks on your current page.

Streaks & Rewards

Consistent performance earns you opportunities to advance more quickly:

  • Streaks — When you write multiple passing summaries or answer several questions correctly in a row, you build an achievement streak. Maintaining streaks can earn you the option to skip certain activities.
  • Exceptional Work — If you write a particularly thorough and insightful summary that exceeds the evaluation criteria, you may be given the opportunity to skip the next summary.
Look for the flame icon on question boxes — it indicates you're currently on a streak!
