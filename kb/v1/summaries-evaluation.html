What is a Page Summary?

A summary writing activity is located at the bottom of some pages. After you read through all the content and answer all short answer questions on the current page, you will be asked to summarize the contents of the page before moving on to the next page.

Not all pages require a summary. Some pages use a Cloze Test as the end-of-page activity instead.

Summary Requirements

A successful summary must meet these criteria:

  • Length: Between 50 and 200 words
  • Language: Written in English
  • Relevance: On topic with the page content
  • Originality: Written in your own words (not copied from the text)

How Evaluation Works

Your summary is automatically evaluated on three key metrics:

Content
Does your summary show good coverage of the page's main points with supporting details?
Language Borrowing
Have you written the summary in your own words or borrowed language from the text?
Relevance
Are the topics and ideas discussed sufficiently relevant to the page content?

Understanding Feedback

If your summary doesn't pass the evaluation, you will receive feedback to help you improve:

1

Review the evaluation result

After submitting, you'll see which metrics passed and which need improvement.

2

Click the Details tab

Access more detailed feedback explaining specifically what to improve.

3

Revise and resubmit

Write a revised summary. Your new summary must be significantly different from the previous one — the Uniqueness bar shows how different your revision is.

Simply making small edits to a failing summary won't work. The system checks that your revision is meaningfully different. Focus on restructuring and rephrasing rather than tweaking individual words.

Re-reading for Improvement

When your summary fails evaluation, iTELL AI may automatically redirect you to a specific section of the page to re-read. This guided re-reading process helps you improve your understanding:

  1. iTELL AI highlights a section of the page you should re-read.
  2. After re-reading, click "I'm ready for question" to answer a comprehension question.
  3. Discuss your answer with iTELL AI to deepen your understanding.
  4. Click "Return to summary" when you're ready to try again.

iTELL AI will only redirect you for re-reading once per page.

Tips for Better Summaries

Best practices for passing summaries:
  • Identify the 2-3 main ideas of the page before writing.
  • Use your own words — paraphrasing is key.
  • Include supporting details, not just topic headings.
  • Stay focused on the page content (don't add outside information).
  • Aim for 100-150 words for a balanced summary.
