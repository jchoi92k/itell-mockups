Constructed Response Items

As you read through a page, you may encounter Constructed Response Items (CRIs) instead of the regular "Continue Reading" button. These are short-answer questions that test your understanding of the section you just read.

Constructed Response Items appear between sections as you read. They are different from the end-of-page summary or cloze test.

Answering Questions

When a Constructed Response Item appears:

1

Read the question

The question will ask you to briefly explain a concept from the text you just read.

2

Write your answer

Provide a brief, focused explanation demonstrating your understanding.

3

Submit

Click Submit. If correct, the next section will be unlocked and unblurred.

When You Get It Wrong

If you submit an incorrect answer, you have several options:

  • Resubmit — Revise your answer and try again.
  • "How can I improve my answer?" — Get more detailed feedback from iTELL AI to help you formulate a better response.
  • Reveal Answer — See the correct answer if you're stuck.
  • Continue Reading — Skip the question and move on (if available).
Tip: Try to answer on your own before using the "Reveal Answer" option. Struggling with the question helps reinforce your learning.

Cloze Tests (Fill-in-the-Blank)

Some pages use a Cloze Test as the end-of-page activity instead of a summary. A Cloze Test is a fill-in-the-blank exercise that appears at the bottom of the page after you finish reading.

1

Read the passage

A passage from the page will appear with several blanks.

2

Fill in each blank

Use your best judgment to fill in the missing words based on what you've read.

3

Submit Answers

Click "Submit Answers" to receive feedback on your responses.

4

Retry if needed

If you didn't get every answer correct, press "Try Again" to revise.
